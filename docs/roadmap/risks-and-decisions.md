## Risk Matrix

### High Severity
| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|------------|
| **Dependency Breaking Changes** (TRL, PEFT, Unsloth) | Training pipeline breaks without warning | High | Version pinning with upper bounds, CI nightly builds against latest deps, compatibility matrix |
| **EU AI Act Non-Compliance** (August 2026 deadline) | Enterprise customers cannot adopt ForgeLM for high-risk AI | High | Phase 8 deep compliance: Annex IV docs, audit log, risk assessment, human gate, data governance |
| **Safety Degradation from Fine-Tuning** | Fine-tuned models lose alignment, enterprise liability | High | Phase 6 safety evaluation pipeline, auto-revert on safety regression |
| **Alignment Method Lock-In** | ForgeLM supports only ORPO while market demands DPO/GRPO | High | Phase 5 is top priority — critical market expectation |
| **Compliance window closing** (Aug 2, 2026) | Enterprise leads not converted before competitors add compliance features | High | Enterprise outreach Day 1; Turkey market as first pilot; deep compliance features (Audit API, managed cloud) as moat |
| **Community flywheel not started** | 0 stars → 0 enterprise credibility → missed EU AI Act window | High | Phase 10.5 Quickstart prioritized; YouTube Academy; Show HN + Reddit launch |

### Medium Severity
| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|------------|
| **MoE/VLM Architecture Shift** | ForgeLM cannot train dominant model architectures | Medium | Phase 7 addresses this; monitor PEFT library MoE support |
| **Scope Creep** (too many trainers, model types) | Maintenance burden exceeds capacity, core quality degrades | Medium | Strict phase gating, leverage TRL's existing trainers |
| **Ecosystem Commoditization** (Axolotl, LLaMA-Factory) | Competing tools add similar enterprise features | Medium | Double down on safety + compliance differentiation |
| **GPU/CUDA Version Fragmentation** | Users on different CUDA versions hit incompatibilities | Medium | Docker images pin CUDA versions, compatibility matrix |

### Low Severity
| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|------------|
| **Model merging adoption uncertainty** | Feature built but rarely used | Low | Implement as separate CLI command, minimal core changes |
| **Notebook template maintenance** | Notebooks break with library updates | Low | Auto-generate from config templates, CI validation |

## Opportunity Analysis

### Immediate Opportunities
1. **Alignment Stack (Phase 5)** — DPO+GRPO support closes the most critical competitive gap. Every serious fine-tuning workflow in 2026 uses preference optimization.
2. **Safety-as-a-Feature (Phase 6)** — No competitor integrates safety evaluation into the training pipeline. ForgeLM can own "the safest way to fine-tune an LLM."

3. **EU AI Act Compliance** — August 2026 deadline creates urgent demand. ForgeLM as the only tool generating compliance artifacts is a powerful enterprise sales argument.
4. **MoE Fine-Tuning** — Qwen3, DeepSeek-V3 dominance means MoE support is becoming table stakes.
5. **Cost Transparency** — GPU cost tracking per run enables enterprise budget planning and optimization. Simple to implement, high perceived value.

### Long-Term Opportunities
6. **Managed ForgeLM Service** — SaaS offering: upload data + config → receive trained model + compliance artifacts.
7. **Synthetic Data Pipeline** — Config-driven teacher model distillation before training. Unique integration no competitor offers.
8. **Training Marketplace** — Community-contributed config templates for common use cases.

### 2026-07-20 — Module-size deferrals re-tracked as budgets (no target version)

> **Source.** `forgelm/safety.py` crossed the ceiling at v0.9.1 and was split into `forgelm/safety/`. Reviewing the remaining entries in `tools/check_module_size.py` surfaced that all eight carried the label **"defer to v0.6.x split"** — written honestly at v0.5.5, still printing at v0.9.1rc1, three minor releases after the named cycle closed. This is the third instance of one rot pattern in this cycle, alongside the deprecation-removal targets that named `v0.9.0` after v0.9.0 shipped and Phase 14.5 still naming the closed `v0.7.x` cycle.

**What the old label cost.** Nothing enforced it, so between PR #29 and v0.9.1 every deferred module *grew*, and the guard emitted the same reassuring WARN throughout. `forgelm/compliance.py` went from ~1500 to 2147 LOC — it was added to during this very cycle — and `forgelm/ingestion.py` from ~1443 to 2110. A module could have reached any size at all without a single fatal signal. Two entries were also simply wrong by 2026-07-20: `forgelm/safety.py` had been split, and `forgelm/cli/subcommands/_doctor.py` had been trimmed to 950 LOC and was under the ceiling, still being reported as over-ceiling debt.

**Decision: record a budget, not a due date.** Each deferred module now pins its measured LOC at deferral time, and the guard enforces a ratchet — a deferred module may stay over the ceiling but may not grow past its budget, fatal in every mode. A budget asserts nothing about the future and therefore cannot become false the way `v0.6.x` did. Two alternatives were rejected:

- **Bump `v0.6.x` to a new version literal.** This is exactly the move that rotted twice already; `tools/check_deprecation_targets.py` exists because a duplicated version literal rotted the same way. It would buy one release cycle of accuracy and then reproduce the problem.
- **A prose-only backlog with no enforcement.** Better than a false label but still WARN-only, which is what let 645 LOC accumulate in `compliance.py` unremarked.

**The honest answer on timing: these seven will not be split soon.** Three minor releases produced zero splits and net growth in every module. Nothing in the current roadmap allocates the ~4-8 h per module the work needs, and `safety.py` was split only because a feature pushed it over the line first. Recording a schedule nobody has committed to is what created this problem; the ratchet holds the line without pretending a date exists. The backlog is therefore **ordered by expected value, not by planned date** — if a release cycle has capacity for one split, take the top row.

**Escape hatch, made explicit.** A deferred module sometimes must grow before it can be split — a security fix in `compliance.py` should not be blocked on a day-long refactor. Raising the budget is allowed, in the same PR, by editing the literal and appending the reason to that entry's `budget_history`. This converts what used to be silent drift into a reviewed line in a diff with a stated justification.

**Removed entries.** `forgelm/safety.py` (split into `forgelm/safety/`) and `forgelm/cli/subcommands/_doctor.py` (950 LOC, back under the ceiling; now held to the normal ceiling like any other module). Both removals are recorded as comments in `_DEFERRED_SPLITS` so they read as deliberate in `git blame` rather than as accidental deletions.

See [`docs/standards/architecture.md`](../standards/architecture.md#module-size-ceiling) for the rule this enforces and `tools/check_module_size.py`'s module docstring for the mechanism.

> **Addendum — 2026-07-20 (same-day review of the mechanism, not a re-decision).** Reviewing the guard just landed above surfaced two places where its *documented* promise was wider than its *enforced* invariant — the same class of defect the section itself was written to correct, one level up. Neither changes the decision; both change what is claimed and what is checked.
>
> 1. **The ratchet's unit is a file, not a concern.** "A deferred module may not grow" is true of the file and false of the concern each `reason` describes: a new 803-LOC sibling module (a `_trainer_overflow.py` dropped beside the deferred `forgelm/trainer.py`) passes `--strict` with exit 0 and the summary still reads "0 NEW over warn-threshold", because 803 is under the ceiling every non-deferred module is held to. This was reproduced, not theorised. The mechanism is deliberately left alone: every `reason` names the sibling files a split should produce, so charging new siblings against the parent's budget would penalise the exact refactor the backlog asks for, and a per-concern budget needs a hand-maintained file-to-concern map — the rot-prone artefact this budget mechanism replaced. Only the claim was narrowed, in the guard docstring, the FAIL message, `architecture.md`, and pinned by a regression test so the claim and the invariant stay one sentence.
> 2. **`budget_history` is now enforced, not merely required.** As shipped it was documented as making "every grant of extra headroom a reviewed line in a diff with a stated justification" while nothing checked it — a bare budget bump passed silently. Each entry now also carries `deferred_at_loc`, the measurement taken at deferral, which unlike `budget` is never edited; `budget > deferred_at_loc` with an empty or blank `budget_history` is fatal in every mode. Residual gap, stated rather than glossed: editing both fields in one commit defeats the check, which is two deliberate falsifications of a field documented as immutable rather than the zero-friction drift the old policy allowed.
